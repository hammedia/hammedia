About

My formal education ended with high school.

But I have never been far from learning. I was accepted to a university in Japan, then returned to Korea during the IMF crisis. In the United States, I was close to enrollment, but life came first. While working at Starbucks, I entered Hanyang Cyber University to study business administration. After one year, I left when studying began to feel like a tool for promotion rather than growth.

That was not giving up. It was choosing reality, again and again.

I believe people are proven more by capability than credentials, and more by wisdom than knowledge. That belief did not come from theory. I learned it in the field.

13 Years at Starbucks — recognized for capability, not credentials

I started as a barista and worked my way through store manager, district manager, talent development manager, and regional operations leader for Incheon. I managed annual revenue in the 20 billion KRW range and led operations with about 200 partners. I turned three loss-making stores profitable and, in 2013, designed training programs for every role level.

During a coffee tasting at the Seattle headquarters, invited by Howard Schultz, I understood something clearly: I wanted to design the experience around coffee, not just serve coffee itself.
Founding Della Casa — turning sensibility into revenue

I planned and operated Italian restaurants in Sinpo-dong and Cheongna, Incheon. That taught me that people do not come only for food. Lighting, music, and the tone of service all connect to return visits, average spend, and reviews. The pandemic forced the restaurants to close, and that weight still gives my decisions a practical sense of reality.

HAM MEDIA — where these experiences come together

Today, I run a one-person media studio under the name HAM MEDIA. Before shooting, I first look at the structure: what kind of customer this expert needs to meet, what that customer is worried about, and what words will make them feel trust.

Good content is not just a polished video.
It is a framework that builds trust, helping customers feel, “I can trust this person.”

I did not learn this from theory. I learned it in Starbucks stores, in the Della Casa kitchen, on the audio sales floor, and at real estate negotiation tables.

If no one else recognizes me, I still need to recognize myself.
So I hired myself, and gave myself the work.
